Crew Leader

 

PURPOSE

To facilitate, bring together and supervise all the pieces needed in order to have a high quality of services delivered in the shortest amount of time possible and in a very organized and smooth way.

MAIN RESPONSIBILITIES

  1. Take the crew to and from each job on time in order to get our services delivered.
  2. Make sure that they have all the tools needed in the van in order to do the job (Chemicals, tools, towels, etc.)
  3. Coordinate everything at the job location in order to have it running smooth.
  4. Supervise the jobs and check the quality of the final job.
  5. Get in communication with the OWNER, MANAGER or person IN CHARGE in order to deliver the invoice and pick up payment on COD jobs.

OTHER RESPONSIBILITIES

  1. Check the van stickers to make sure that they are up-to-date.
  2. Check when the oil changes are needed and take the van to the mechanic to have it done.
  3. Wash the van to keep it clean.
  4. Make sure that at ALL times the van is fully equipped and operational.
  5. Perform other duties as assigned.
Teamwork and coordination of efforts are vital in this position.  As the head of the team, you will have to have good people skills. Having a crew that works in synergy, as a team depends on you.

TECHNICAL COMPETENCIES

  1. Driver license type C
  2. Good driving record.
  3. Bilingual (English and Spanish).

SKILLS

Initiative: You will have to help the crew on the cleaning when needed; you will have to accommodate to play the role of reinforcement whenever needed in order to finish a job.
Prediction:  the ability to estimate real travel time (due to distance, traffic, time of day, weather, etc.), how long a job is going to take to complete at the current rate of work being done, how many resources are going to be needed at a job site, etc. 
Good communication:  You need to supervise, delegate and in general deal with clients and employees, therefore good communication and good inter-personal skills are vital.
Commitment: You would need to be able to understand what is expected from you as well as the load that you will carry and make it happen.
Responsibility: A good sense of responsibility is needed due to the level of importance of this position. You have to be willing to make things go right whatever the situation.

VFP - VALUABLE FINAL PRODUCT - (EXPECTED OUTCOME)

  1. Smooth performance of the group through the right coordination and supervision.
  2. Jobs delivered on time and finished in the shortest amount of time possible.
  3. Satisfied customers with no complains and quality issues to resolve, and ready to recommend our services to others.
  4. A fully operating van at all times with little to no replacement of tools.

THINGS TO AVOID

  1. Driving over the speed limits.
  2. Not having all and/or the correct invoices needed for the jobs.
  3. Not having the correct tool(s) for the job.
  4. Giving the impression of unreliability to the customers and the crew.

THINGS TO CONSIDER

  1. The safety of the vehicle at all times. That includes parking when at a job.
  2. An extra set of vehicle keys available at all times.
